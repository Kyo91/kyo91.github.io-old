Migrating To Hugo With Org

Thu Feb 15, 2018 by in  meta, hugo

I have recently moved over my blog from Jekyll to Hugo. I can’t really say I have a great reason for doing so, but as this blog has no traffic and only one post, I figured I had nothing to lose from doing so. That being said, I found myself not liking my defaults on Jekyll while also feeling unfamiliar with it’s layout, structure and ruby internals so that I didn’t feel comfortable changing things.

While Hugo doesn’t offer me any advantages in these regards, it does seem to have great 3rd party emacs org support through ox-hugo which allows me to create all my posts as one org-mode file and publish them separately. Honestly, I was initially looking for a direct org → static site solution, but upon being unable to find anything that fit my needs, I stumbled upon ox-hugo and it seems to fulfill all my requirements.
